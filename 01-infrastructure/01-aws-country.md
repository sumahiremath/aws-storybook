# The Country of AWS

## Why Does AWS Have So Many Services?

---

The traveler arrived carrying nothing more than an application.

At the border stood an old guide holding an enormous map.

"First time?" the guide asked.
The traveler nodded.

"I only came here to run my application."
The guide smiled.

"So did everyone else."
They walked through the gates.

The traveler stared in disbelief.
There were highways stretching beyond the horizon.

Warehouses taller than mountains.
Banks carved into stone.
A postal service moving millions of messages.
Airports connecting distant lands.
Hospitals preparing for disasters.
Power stations humming beneath the city.

Everywhere the traveler looked...
another government department.

Finally they stopped.

"I don't understand."
"I thought AWS was cloud computing."

The guide laughed.

"It is."

"So why is it an entire country?"

The guide folded the map.

"Because every building you see exists because someone needed it."

He pointed toward the post office.
"Messages used to get lost."

The bank.
"People lost valuable data."

The highways.
"Applications grew."

The hospitals.
"Disasters happened."

The airports.
"The world became connected."

The traveler looked across the skyline.

"So..."

"None of this is random?"

The guide smiled.

"No."

"For twenty years, engineers arrived with problems."

"The country simply kept building departments."

---

**Knife Cut**

AWS isn't a collection of services.

It's a country built from twenty years of engineering lessons.
